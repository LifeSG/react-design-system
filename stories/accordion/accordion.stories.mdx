<Title>Accordion</Title>

<Secondary>Overview</Secondary>

Displays expandable and collapsible content panels that can be toggled by the user.

```tsx
import { Accordion } from "@lifesg/react-design-system/accordion";
```

<Canvas>
    <Story name="Accordion">
        <Accordion title="My Accordion">
            <Accordion.Item title="This is the first item">
                <Text.Body>
                    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit, sed
                    do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna
                    aliqua. Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation
                    ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat.
                </Text.Body>
            </Accordion.Item>
            <Accordion.Item title="This is the second item">
                <Text.Body>
                    Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit, sed
                    do eiusmod tempor&nbsp;
                    <Text.Hyperlink.Default
                        href="https://www.google.com"
                        target="_blank"
                    >
                        see more here
                    </Text.Hyperlink.Default>.
                </Text.Body>
            </Accordion.Item>
        </Accordion>
    </Story>
</Canvas>

<Heading3>Smaller Accordion item titles</Heading3>

Specifying the type in the `Accordion.Item` produces a smaller text size for each item's title.

<Heading3>Showing title in mobile view</Heading3>

By default, the `Accordion` will not render the title in mobile views. However if you require, you may
use the `showTitleInMobile` prop to enable the title to be visible in mobile view.

<Preview>
    <Story name="Title shown in mobile">
        <Accordion title="Title in mobile too" showTitleInMobile>
            <Accordion.Item title="This is the first item">
                <Text.Body>

                    do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna

        </Accordion>
    </Story>
</Preview>
